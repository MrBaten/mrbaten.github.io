Below are my research publications on artificial intelligence, deep learning, and machine learning, featured in international journals and conferences.

RHMCD-20 Dataset: Identify Rapid Human Mental Health Depression During Quarantine Life Using Machine Learning.

The RHMCD-20 dataset offers a thorough investigation of the dynamics of mental health in Bangladesh while under quarantine. The structured survey that was distributed to different demographic groups yielded a dataset that included a wide range of variables, such as age, gender, occupation, and stress levels.

Real-Time Medical Image Classification with ML Framework and Dedicated CNN–LSTM Architecture

In the domain of modern deep learning and classification techniques, the convolutional neural network (CNN) stands out as a highly successful and preferred method for image classification in artificial intelligence.

AutoML: A Systematic Review on Automated Machine Learning with Neural Architecture Search

AutoML (Automated Machine Learning) is an emerging field that aims to automate the process of building machine learning models. AutoML emerged to increase productivity and efficiency by automating as much as possible the inefficient work that occurs while repeating this process whenever machine learning is applied.


Improve CNN Model Agro-Crop Leaf Disease Identification Based on Transfer Learning

In this study, we have constructed an integrated model based on improved VGG16 and ResNet-50 network. For the improvement of CNN architecture, VGG16 full connected layer drops then it is connected with the next full collected layer due to reduce the computing complexity.

Hybrid Convolution Neural Network with Transfer Learning Approach for Agro-Crop Leaf Disease Identification

Transfer learning is an optimization that assumes faster development and superior performance when modeling a second task. In this study, we have constructed an integrated model based on improved VGG16 and ResNet-50 network.

Deep Learning Models for Real-Time Identification of Agro Plant Insects with Image Augmentation Effect Analysis on a Small Custom Dataset

In our study, we investigate the impact of six distinct augmentation techniques on a limited dataset. We manually gathered data from the agricultural plant insect dataset (BAU-Insectv2) and employed four state-of-the-art CNN architectures (ResNet-101-v2, EfficientNet-B1, Inception v3 and Inception- ResNet v1 ) with various optimizers and augmentation techniques.
